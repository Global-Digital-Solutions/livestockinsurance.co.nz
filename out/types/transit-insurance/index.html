About Livestock Transit Insurance in New Zealand

Every time livestock leave your property — whether for a routine trip to the saleyards, transport to another farm, or the journey to a processing plant — they face risks that your standard farm policy may not cover. Livestock transit insurance is the specific cover that protects your animals during these periods of movement, when they are most vulnerable.

Transit deaths and injuries are more common than most farmers anticipate. Vehicle accidents on rural roads, heat stress during summer transport, loading and unloading injuries, equipment failures, and animals going down in transit are all genuine risks. For high-value stud animals — bulls worth $10,000–$50,000, registered dairy cows, or prize velvet stags — a single transit loss without insurance can be devastating.

The coverage provided by transit insurance varies between insurers. At its most basic, it covers the death of animals during transport. More comprehensive policies extend cover to the time animals spend in saleyards or at agricultural events, injuries sustained during loading and unloading, theft from trucks or yards while in transit, and cover for animals at their destination before the return journey.

For stud breeders who regularly travel to shows and sales around New Zealand, transit cover is especially important. A champion ram or prize bull on the showground is as valuable as it is on the farm — and more exposed. Some specialist policies can cover animals at agricultural shows for the duration of an event.

A licensed rural insurance adviser can identify whether transit cover is included in your existing farm pack policy (it often is, but with limits) or whether a standalone transit policy would better suit your needs, and help you get quotes from multiple providers.

What Does Livestock Transit Insurance Cover?

Road Transport Accidents

Death or injury to animals resulting from vehicle accidents, collisions, overturning, or equipment failure during transport by road.

Loading & Unloading Injuries

Injuries sustained when animals are being loaded onto or unloaded from trucks, including fractures, dislocations, and soft tissue injuries.

Deaths During Transit

Animals that die during the journey — including heat stress deaths, going down in transit, and injuries sustained during transport.

Saleyard & Event Cover

Some policies extend cover to the time animals spend in saleyards, holding yards, or at agricultural events before and after the journey.

Theft During Transit

Animals stolen from trucks, trailers, or holding yards while in transit — increasingly important as organised livestock theft increases in NZ.

Rail & Coastal Transport

Cover during transport by rail or coastal shipping — relevant for South Island to North Island livestock movements.

Common Exclusions to Be Aware Of

  • Deaths caused by disease contracted before or during transit (covered under disease & illness policy)
  • Transit in vehicles that are not roadworthy or do not comply with animal welfare requirements
  • Animals not listed on the policy schedule or not declared at the time of transit
  • Losses arising from the farmer's own negligence or failure to comply with animal welfare standards during loading
  • Deaths during unregulated or illegal transport arrangements
  • Cover when animals are moved beyond the geographic limits specified in the policy

A licensed rural insurance adviser can explain exactly what is and isn't covered under any policy before you commit.

Who Needs Livestock Transit Insurance?

  • Farmers who regularly send stock to sales
  • Stud breeders who show animals
  • Deer farmers who transport velvet stags
  • Anyone moving high-value animals by road or rail

How Much Does Livestock Transit Insurance Cost?

Livestock transit insurance premiums are typically calculated per trip (for occasional transport) or as an annual policy for farmers who transport stock regularly. Per-trip cover for a load of sheep to the saleyards might cost $20–$80. Annual transit policies for farms that regularly move stock can cost $200–$1,500+ depending on stock values, frequency of transport, and the species and number of animals involved. Stud animal transit cover for high-value individuals is priced separately, often as a percentage of the animal's agreed value per journey.

Frequently Asked Questions — Livestock Transit Insurance

Does my farm policy automatically cover livestock during transport?

Many farm pack policies include some transit cover, but the scope and limits vary. Standard farm policies often cover transport to the nearest saleyard or processing plant, but may not cover animals during extended stays at agricultural shows, transport interstate, or theft from vehicles. It's important to check your policy wording carefully and discuss with your adviser.

Do I need to declare each individual transport movement to my insurer?

For annual transit policies, typically no — routine transport is automatically covered within the policy parameters. For per-trip transit cover or for high-value individual animals, you may need to notify your insurer before each journey. Your adviser will explain the notification requirements of your specific policy.

What should I do if animals are injured or die during transport?

Retain all documentation — the transport contractor's details, the route taken, the time of travel, and any veterinary certificates. Notify your insurer as soon as possible. Do not dispose of animals before a claim has been registered. Take photographs if possible. Your adviser can guide you through the claims process.

Can I get transit cover for high-value stud animals attending shows?

Yes — specialist livestock transit and show cover can be arranged for stud animals attending agricultural events. This typically covers the animal from departure, throughout the show, and during return transport. The animal's agreed value (its show or breeding value) can be insured for this purpose. Talk to your adviser before the event.

Is transit insurance required by law in NZ?

Transit insurance is not legally required in New Zealand. However, it is strongly recommended, particularly for high-value animals. If you use a professional livestock transport contractor, check whether their public liability insurance covers the value of your animals — usually it does not cover the full market value of your stock.
