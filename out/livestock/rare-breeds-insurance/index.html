About Rare Breeds & Show Animals in New Zealand

New Zealand has a small but dedicated community of rare breed and heritage livestock keepers — from Highland and Belted Galloway cattle to Kunekune pigs, Valais Blacknose sheep, Babydoll Southdown, rare Angora goats, and show-quality alpacas. These animals occupy a unique position in the livestock world: their value derives not from commercial production output but from genetic rarity, breed preservation role, show records, and the community of enthusiasts who have invested in them.

Standard livestock insurance — blanket mob policies priced at commercial market replacement rates — is fundamentally unsuitable for rare breed animals. A registered Highland bull with show ribbons and a documented pedigree cannot be "replaced" at the price of a commercial Angus cross. A Kunekune boar from founding bloodlines may have no commercial replacement at any price. Agreed value specified cover, arranged by brokers who understand non-standard livestock valuation, is the only appropriate insurance approach for these animals.

Show animals — whatever their breed — face specific transit risks that require careful insurance planning. The show circuit involves regular transport to A&P shows, breed society events, and national championships. Comprehensive transit cover that applies from loading at the home property through the entire show visit and return journey is essential for active show competitors.

Gallagher Insurance is the most experienced mainstream broker in New Zealand for exotic and non-standard livestock. Aon can access specialist underwriters at Lloyd's of London for truly unusual or high-value animals where the local market has limited appetite.

This guide covers rare breed and show animal insurance in New Zealand: what is covered, how agreed value is established, exclusions, how to compare brokers, and what documentation to maintain.

What Does Rare Breeds & Show Animals Cover?

Agreed Value Specified Cover

The core of rare breed insurance. Each animal is insured at a fixed agreed value set at policy inception — supported by purchase records, registration papers, show results, pedigree documentation, and independent valuations. If the animal dies from a covered cause, the full agreed value is paid, regardless of commercial replacement cost.

Transit Cover — Show Circuit

Covers animals from loading at your property through transport, time at the show venue, and return journey. Loading/unloading injuries, road accidents, and stress-related illness during transit are covered. Multi-day show attendance may require a specific endorsement.

Accidental Death

Standard mortality cover for death from accidents — electrocution, drowning, fire, vehicle collision, falls, or other accidental causes. For rare breed animals, the claim is paid at the agreed value rather than market replacement cost.

Theft

Rare and high-value animals can be attractive theft targets. Theft cover compensates at agreed value for confirmed stolen animals. Registration papers, microchipping where applicable, and property-level security measures support claims.

Disease & Illness

Covers losses from specified diseases, including emergency vet costs and forced culling. Disease events are particularly damaging for small rare breed herds or flocks where a single death significantly impacts the breeding programme.

Show Award & Championship Cover

Some specialist policies extend to cover loss of use or earning potential for champion show animals generating breeding or straw income based on show reputation. A niche product but relevant for high-profile show animals.

Common Exclusions to Be Aware Of

  • Death where the agreed value cannot be supported by documentation at policy inception
  • Pre-existing conditions or known health issues
  • Losses from neglect, starvation, or failure to provide appropriate care for the specific breed
  • Death during procedures not performed to accepted veterinary standards
  • Gradual deterioration, old age, or reproductive failure
  • Economic decisions to cull animals that are alive but no longer breeding

A licensed rural insurance adviser can help you understand exactly what is and isn't covered under any policy before you commit.

How Much Does Rare Breeds & Show Animals Cost?

Rare breed and show animal insurance premiums are typically 2–4% of the agreed annual value, reflecting the non-standard nature of the livestock and the difficulty of replacement. A $5,000 registered Highland cow might cost $100–$200/year. A $15,000 show-winning heritage pig might cost $300–$600/year. Transit cover for show circuit animals adds a modest per-trip or annual premium. Specialist agreed value cover from brokers accessing Lloyd's markets may carry higher premiums but provides more certain protection for truly unusual animals.

Frequently Asked Questions — Rare Breeds & Show Animals

What breed society registrations support agreed value cover?

Registration with the relevant breed society — Highland Cattle Society of NZ, NZ Kunekune Pig Society, NZ Alpaca Association, or other bodies — provides documented pedigree records that support agreed value claims. Show records from breed society events are additional evidence of value.

Can I insure a Kunekune pig at its breeding value?

Yes. Registered Kunekune pigs from founding or heritage bloodlines can be insured at agreed value reflecting their breeding programme value rather than their commercial meat value. This requires a specialist broker such as Gallagher Insurance and documentation of the animal's bloodlines and breeding record.

Do I need to microchip my rare breed animals for insurance?

Microchipping is not universally required for livestock insurance, but it provides definitive identification that assists with theft recovery and death claims. For rare breeds where individual identity is important, microchipping alongside registration papers creates a strong documentation set.

Are Valais Blacknose sheep insurable in New Zealand?

Yes. Valais Blacknose sheep can be insured at agreed value through specialist brokers. Given their recent importation and the cost of embryo import programmes, animals can have values well above standard commercial sheep. Gallagher Insurance and Aon are the appropriate brokers.

What if my rare breed animal's agreed value is disputed at claim time?

Agreed value policies are specifically designed to avoid this scenario. The value is agreed and documented at policy inception — it is not subject to re-negotiation at claim time, unlike indemnity-based policies. Maintaining good documentation at the time the policy is set is essential for rare breed owners.
